Orgin Of SainLarent






Saint Laurent, a fashion brand launched in Paris by designer Yves Saint Laurent in 1961, renamed it Saint Laurent from Yves Saint Laurent in 2012. Saint Laurent started the brand with Haut Couture, a high-end custom outfit that was a typical fashion form of the 1960s, but introduced the ready-to-wear brand "Rive Gauche" in 1966, introducing a young street sensibility to fashion at the time when it was all about elegance. Saint Laurent became a fashion designer and brand along with Christian Dior and Coco Chanel in the 20th century, presenting "Le Smoking," which is considered to have freed women from gender discrimination, and "Mondrian Collection," a representative example of introducing art into fashion.

Fashion Revolution




the introduction of works of art into fashion


In 1965, Yves Saint Laurent presented the Mondrian dress in the fall and winter collections. The Mondrian dress is a print version of Piet Mondrian's "Composition with Red, Yellow and Blue," a September 1965 edition of Harper's Bazaar, a collection of distinctly white dresses. The Mondrian dress was recorded as the most photographed garment in the history of the fashion magazine, and it was Yves Saint Laurent's chance to make sure his name was recognized in the world.
